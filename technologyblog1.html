Technology: Boon or Bane?


Sujina Dangal

January 3, 2023



The word technology comprises a fairly broad scope, which can make it difficult to pinpoint an exact definition for it, but commonly it can be considered as any scientific knowledge put to use for a practical purpose. Technology has been an integral part of human life for centuries, but the development of digital technologies over the past few decades has drastically changed the way we live and interact with one another. With this, we must ask whether technology is truly a boon or a bane.


Technology has greatly revolutionized the way we live our lives, enabling us to connect and communicate with people from all over the world in an instant and facilitating the sharing of information and ideas across borders and cultures. The technological revolution has undeniably altered the way of life for everyone, but there is no exception that it is a double-edged sword like everything else.


From the advent of the internet to the development of artificial intelligence, technology is ubiquitous in our lives. Technology has made the world a much smaller place, opening a whole new realm of opportunities for those who have access to it and enabling people to reach new heights of innovation and efficiency. By increasing our approach to knowledge and resources, technology has enhanced education and opened up new industries and chances for economic growth. Along with the development of technology, opportunities for growth and collaboration have expanded, enabling us to work together to find solutions to global problems. Plenty of medical advancements such as artificial intelligence and robotics have enabled us to diagnose and treat diseases more accurately and with greater efficiency, resulting in improved patient outcomes. It has enabled us to explore new horizons, from the depth of the ocean to outer space. The power of technology to improve lives and expand our horizons is evident in the growth of communications and transportation, the advances in medicine and education, the explosion of media outlets, and the global reach of entertainment. Because technology has become such a crucial aspect of our everyday life, it can be daunting to even fathom a future without technology. Regardless of how magnificent it is, it is important to remember that technology is not infallible and can have some serious negative impacts on our lives.


For instance, the rise of digital technology has led to a decrease in social interaction. People are often so reserved in their devices that they forget to pay attention to the people around them. The ever-growing obsession with different social media platforms has made people so absorbed in their virtual lives that they are often disconnected from their real lives and the people around them. People are so immersed in faking a perfect life on their social media that they forget to actually live and are the victims of anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues. Additionally, technology has also led to an increase in cybercrime, as hackers can use technology to gain access to private information and commit various types of digital fraud. Cyberbullying and harassment are at their highest peak; countless people are losing their lives as a result. Millions of people could be out of work within the next few years and would have to find new careers that do not require human intelligence. The World Economic Forum's Future of Jobs Report predicts that many jobs will be replaced by computers and artificial intelligence in a few decades' time. Furthermore, technology has also had a detrimental effect on the environment as the production of electronic goods often involves the use of hazardous chemicals which are then released into the environment, contributing to the production of dangerous pollutants and toxic wastes, which can have long-term effects on the planet’s health. This is further compounded by the excessive and unnecessary use of electricity and the improper disposal of electronic goods which leads to e-waste. The carbon footprint of technology is significantly increasing due to the high production, manufacturing, and usage of electronic gadgets.


Although the technological breakthrough that has occurred over the past few decades has been beneficial, it has also raised various concerns. These concerns need to be addressed in order for society to reap the full benefits of technology. As technology continues to advance, it is essential to understand the potential consequences of relying too heavily on technology and its impact on the environment. In conclusion, while technology can be a great boon, it can also be a bane if not used responsibly. It is therefore of utmost importance to be mindful of how we use technology and its potential effects on our lives. We must strive to minimize the inevitable banes so that we may ensure the optimization of the boons to the best of our abilities.
